# Product Requirements Document (PRD) - AstroSim

**Product Name**: AstroSim  
**Version**: 0.1 (MVP)  
**Date**: June 29, 2026  

## 1. Vision
An open-source, modular, AI-native simulation framework for modeling, analyzing, and optimizing sustainable human and robotic presence in space — lunar bases, Mars colonies, orbital habitats, and deep-space missions.

## 2. Problem
Existing tools are fragmented, proprietary, or lack modern AI integration. This slows progress in space colonization and limits community participation and scientific discovery.

## 3. Target Users
- Aerospace and space engineers
- Researchers and academics
- Students and educators
- Space enthusiasts and colonization advocates

## 4. Core Goals
- Provide a free, extensible platform for system-level space habitat simulation.
- Accelerate engineering decisions and scientific discovery for multi-planetary life.
- Become the standard open framework for space sustainability modeling.

## 5. Key Features (MVP)
- Modular subsystem models (Power, ECLSS, Thermal, Structure, ISRU, Compute/AI).
- Time-stepped simulation engine with Monte Carlo capabilities.
- Energy, mass, and reliability budgeting.
- Basic AI integration (LLM hooks for optimization and insights).
- Visualization dashboards and export.
- Example scenarios (lunar base, Mars habitat).

## 6. Non-Goals (MVP)
- Real-time 3D rendering or hardware-in-the-loop.
- High-fidelity CFD/FEA (integrate externally).
- Classified data support.

## 7. Success Metrics
- Functional MVP with runnable examples.
- Active GitHub community.
- Used in at least one published study or project within 6 months.

## 8. Assumptions
- Python-based for accessibility.
- Uses public data and open libraries.
- Community will help extend it over time.
